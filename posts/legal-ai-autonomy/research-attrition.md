# Research: Young Lawyer Attrition in Singapore

## Summary

Young lawyer attrition in Singapore is a critical, long-standing problem that connects directly to the autonomy/satisfaction themes. **60% of newly minted lawyers (2024-2025) say they're likely to leave within 5 years**, with excessive workload, poor work-life balance, and lack of autonomy as primary drivers.

---

## Key Statistics

### Most Recent Data (January 2026)

**Source:** [Law Minister and Chief Justice to co-lead committee to tackle lawyer attrition rates](https://www.singaporelawwatch.sg/Headlines/law-minister-and-chief-justice-to-co-lead-committee-to-tackle-lawyer-attrition-rates-impact-of-ai) - Straits Times, Jan 13, 2026

**Critical Finding:**
- **60% of newly minted lawyers** (2024 and 2025 surveys) said they were **likely to leave legal practice in the next five years**
- Government response: Law Minister Edwin Tong and Chief Justice Sundaresh Menon co-leading committee to address attrition and AI impact

**Top Reasons for Leaving (2024-2025 surveys):**
1. Excessive workload or poor work-life balance
2. Higher salary or compensation package elsewhere
3. Impact work had on their mental well-being
4. Lack of flexibility in working arrangements
5. Poor workplace culture

### 2021 Record Exits

**Source:** [The Big Read: Burnt out and disillusioned, young lawyers head for the exit](https://www.channelnewsasia.com/today/big-read/lawyers-singapore-burn-out-great-resignation-wave-2453276) - CNA, Jan 24, 2022

**Key Statistics:**
- **Record 538 lawyers left the profession in 2021**
- **310 were junior lawyers** (less than 5 years experience)
- Junior category exits represent **14% of all junior category lawyers**
- **Nearly 60% of 2021 exits** were junior lawyers

**Context:**
- Law Society President Adrian Tan called this a record high
- Coincided with record low number of new lawyers called to the bar in 2021

### Historical Context

**Source:** [Revisiting the Attrition of Young Lawyers in Singapore](https://lawgazette.com.sg/practice/practice-support/revisiting-the-attrition-of-young-lawyers-in-singapore) - Singapore Law Gazette

**Long-standing pattern:**
- **2006:** 58% of lawyers with <7 years experience left profession
- **2021:** About 60% of lawyers with <5 years experience left profession
- **Comparison to other jurisdictions:**
  - USA: 18% associate attrition rate (2012-2018)
  - UK: 14% attrition rate during pandemic
  - US: 16% attrition rate during pandemic
  - **Singapore's rate is significantly higher than UK/US**

**Key insight:** This is NOT a pandemic-era "Great Resignation" phenomenon. Singapore has had high young lawyer attrition for at least 15-20 years.

## Explanations for Why Young Lawyers Leave

### Primary Push Factors (from interviews and surveys)

**From CNA Big Read (2022):**

1. **Burnout and excessive hours**
   - Working 9am to 4am routinely
   - 13-hour days at high intensity
   - "Sleep for five hours and then rinse and repeat"

2. **Toxic work culture**
   - Poor management and lack of support
   - Sexist and misogynistic behavior
   - "Office house-work" delegated to women
   - Seniors lacking empathy and patience

3. **Technology paradox**
   - Digitalization makes lawyers more efficient but "victims of their own success"
   - Cramming more work into same hours
   - Clients expect lawyers "on demand, round the clock"
   - Virtual hearings eliminated mental breaks (used to sit in courtroom for 1-2 hours before hearing)

4. **Lack of autonomy and control**
   - Junior lawyers quote: "For me, it comes down to whether the demands of the job are an acceptable cost of being able to do meaningful work"
   - No clear path to future, just fear of reprimand
   - "Piecemeal reactions when a partner chases after you" vs. holistic understanding of tasks

5. **Mismatch of expectations**
   - Many law students are "straight As" students with little interest in actual practice
   - Unrealistic expectations from media/movies about glamorous life
   - Reality: "Most of lawyering involves hard work and frequently disappointing outcomes"

### Structural/Psychological Factors

**From Law Gazette article (referencing academic research):**

Research paper: "Why Lawyers are Unhappy" by Martin E.P. Seligman, Paul R. Verkuil & Terry H. Kang (2005)

**Root causes of unhappiness:**
1. **Pessimism:** Lawyers make good lawyers because they're pessimistic
2. **Poor decision-making latitude:** Lawyers don't make decisions and have little control in their occupation
3. **Zero-sum nature:** For you to win, someone has to lose

**Remedies suggested:**
- Create culture of "learned optimism"
- Increase decision-making latitude for junior lawyers
- Empower juniors to shape their environment
- Cooperation to mitigate adversarial system effects

## Connection to Work Satisfaction and Autonomy

### Direct Quotes Linking to Autonomy

**From interviews (CNA, 2022):**

- "For me, it comes down to whether the demands of the job are an acceptable cost of being able to do meaningful work" - Ng Yuan Siang, 29, associate
- Juniors leaving for in-house roles that promise "better work-life balance" and removal from "pressures of private practice like having to hit billable targets"
- "I just felt very alienated. I had a good hard think about what I wanted to do, and this wasn't it." - David, 28

**From Law Gazette analysis:**

> "We all seek meaning, content and pride in the work that we do. To derive meaning and pride from our work, a holistic view of one's tasks must be had, as opposed to being at the receiving end of piecemeal reactions when a partner chases after you."

> "Poor decision-making latitude (i.e. they don't make decisions and have no or little control in their occupation)"

### The Autonomy Gap

**What young lawyers lack:**
1. **Control over workflow:** "Piecemeal reactions when a partner chases after you"
2. **Decision-making authority:** Low decision-making latitude combined with high pressure
3. **Meaningful work:** Can't see holistic purpose, only receive fragmented tasks
4. **Flexibility:** Rigid working arrangements, always on-call for clients

**What they seek:**
1. In-house roles with better work-life balance
2. Non-legal careers entirely (technology, investments, business)
3. Work where they feel capable and in control

## Singapore-Specific Context

✅ **Singapore/ASEAN-Relevant Data**

### Government Response (2026)

- **Committee formation:** Law Minister Edwin Tong + Chief Justice Sundaresh Menon co-leading
- **Committee composition:** Judiciary, AGC, Ministry of Law, Singapore Academy of Law, Law Society, Singapore Corporate Counsel Association, academia
- **Dual challenge:** Attrition + AI impact on legal work

### Law Society Initiatives

**From CNA (2022):**
- LawSoc kicked off "nationwide conversation about the legal profession" on social media, chat groups, private discussions
- Hearing from those who stayed, left, and returned to private practice

**From LinkedIn (Jan 2025):**
- Law Society conducting "Attrition Rate Study survey" to understand the problem

### Industry Responses

**Suggested solutions from senior lawyers (CNA, 2022):**

1. **Set guidelines on maximum hours:** ~45 hours/week average (not hard stop)
2. **Publish salary transparency:** Annual publication of profit per partner and salaries at every level (like UK/US)
3. **Anonymous whistleblowing hotline:** For exploitative and abusive firms
4. **Mandatory management training:** Legal practice management courses now include leadership/management modules
5. **Annual work plan conferences:** One-on-one sessions with juniors about personal and professional goals

## How This Connects to Legal AI Autonomy Post

### The Alignment

**Aslan Legal research (global):**
- Autonomy and trust: 53% (top satisfaction driver)
- Lawyers 7x more likely to enjoy work because of autonomy than bonuses

**Singapore young lawyer attrition (local):**
- 60% likely to leave within 5 years
- Top reasons: excessive workload, poor work-life balance, **lack of flexibility**, poor workplace culture
- Underlying issue: **poor decision-making latitude, no control over occupation**

### The Singapore Angle for Your Intro

You could open with:

> "In Singapore, 60% of newly minted lawyers say they're likely to leave the profession within five years. The reasons? Excessive workload, poor work-life balance, lack of flexibility. But here's what the data actually shows: it's not about hours worked - it's about **control**."

Or:

> "Singapore has a lawyer attrition crisis. In 2024-2025 surveys, 60% of young lawyers said they plan to leave within five years. The government just formed a committee - co-led by the Law Minister and Chief Justice - to address it. But the solutions being discussed (maximum hour guidelines, salary transparency, whistleblowing hotlines) miss what Aslan Legal research shows matters 7x more than bonuses: autonomy."

### The Powerful Connection

- **Global research:** Autonomy is #1 driver of lawyer satisfaction
- **Singapore reality:** Young lawyers leaving because of lack of control, poor decision-making latitude, being at "receiving end of piecemeal reactions"
- **The gap:** Industry focuses on hours worked, pay, and work-life balance - but underlying issue is **autonomy**
- **AI's role:** Can AI provide the autonomy junior lawyers desperately need?

## Alt-Counsel Angle

**For solo counsels and small teams:**

The attrition research shows that even lawyers in structured firms with colleagues and support systems are leaving because of **lack of autonomy and control**.

For solo practitioners and small teams:
- You already lack institutional support
- You already work alone with limited resources
- **Autonomy isn't a nice-to-have - it's essential for survival**

The question: Can AI provide the autonomy that traditional firm structures fail to deliver?
