# Pitch: MinLaw AI Guide — Draft to Final

## Title
From Draft to Final: What Changed in MinLaw's AI Guide (And What It Means for You)

## Pitch
Last September I wrote a blog post critiquing MinLaw's draft Guide for Using Generative AI in the Legal Sector. When I shared it on LinkedIn, something unexpected happened — people encouraged me to submit it formally. So I turned my critique into a four-page submission the night before the consultation closed. Six months later, the final guide landed, and it's a substantially different document. The draft was a 26-page outline that told lawyers to "review, analyse, and verify" AI output but never mentioned AI literacy, treated consumer tools as an edge case, and offered no templates. The final version is a 48-page framework with a new AI literacy requirement, detailed consumer tool protocols, sample governance policies, vendor checklists, and a risk-based oversight model. Some changes track my submission closely. This post walks through the key differences between draft and final, what the consultation process actually changed, and whether the guide now works for solo counsels and small teams.
