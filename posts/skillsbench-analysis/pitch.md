## Title
What 7,308 Agent Runs Taught Me About Writing Better Skills

## Pitch
I thought I was getting good at writing skills. Then my codraft project started failing on a task Claude could already do — because my skill told it to do it wrong. That experience — a custom docx-to-pdf instruction conflicting with Claude's built-in capability — turned out to be one of the most common failure modes identified in SkillsBench, the first large-scale benchmark measuring how Agent Skills actually perform. The paper tested 84 tasks across 7,308 trajectories and found that curated skills improve performance by 16 percentage points on average. But 19% of tasks got *worse* with skills. And AI-generated skills? Useless — or actively harmful. This post connects my experience building skills in codraft and this blog with what the research confirms: focused beats comprehensive, some tasks don't need skills at all, and the gap between models means testing matters more than you think. Whether you're writing your first skill or debugging your fiftieth, here's a practitioner's framework for getting them right.
