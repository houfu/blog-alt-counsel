## CRITICAL: Fact-Check Corrections for Draft

### 1. Zhang's copyright case was against a HUMAN painter, not AI

The draft (v5, "Eyes closed, eyes open" section) currently reads:
> "In 2025, someone ran one of Zhang's high-end fantasy portraits through OpenAI's image model... Zhang fought and won a landmark copyright case in Luxembourg."

**Problem:** This sequences the OpenAI incident (March 2025) and the Luxembourg case as if they're related. They are **separate events**:
- **Luxembourg case (2022-2024):** Zhang sued painter Jeff Dieschburg, who copied her photograph of model Ji Hye Park (Harper's Bazaar Vietnam, 2017) into an oil painting called "Turandot." District court ruled against Zhang in Dec 2022 (said work "lacked originality"). Zhang won on APPEAL in May 2024. The infringer was a human using traditional media (painting), not AI.
- **OpenAI incident (March 2025):** Someone separately ran Zhang's photo through OpenAI's image model. Zhang wrote about feeling "sick" seeing it. This was an img2img conversion, unrelated to the court case.

**Suggested fix:** Either separate these clearly in the text, or acknowledge the case was about a human plagiarist (which actually *strengthens* the post's argument — the plagiarist was intentional, used a paintbrush not AI, and it was still infringement).

### 2. Zhang is Singaporean

The draft doesn't mention this, but Zhang is Singaporean (born in Singapore, based in Seattle/NY). This is a strong regional hook for the blog's audience.

### 3. The TWS comic date

The LinkedIn post is dated March 1, 2026. The draft says "March 2026" — this is accurate.

### Section 4: Intentionality (philosophical grounding)
**Claim being supported:** "AI doesn't make this choice. It resolves a statistical pattern... Deciding isn't something AI does badly. It's something AI doesn't do."

**Evidence found:**
- **Franz Brentano** (1874): Defined intentionality as the "aboutness" of mental states — a quality intrinsic to human consciousness. Mental states are always *about* something. This is the foundational concept. | Regional Context: 🌍 Global/philosophical
- **PhilArchive paper (Ngaihlian, 2025):** "While current AI systems excel at simulating intelligence, they fall short of true intentionality due to their lack of lived experience." Argues current AI cannot transcend algorithmic foundations to embody genuine intentionality. | Regional Context: 🌍 Global
  - Source: [Machine Learning Algorithms: Simulating Intentionality in AI](https://philarchive.org/rec/NGAMLA) - PhilArchive/SSRN (2025)
- **Husserl's phenomenology:** Human intentionality is "a unique and vital aspect of consciousness that sets us apart." AI may achieve impressive feats but cannot replicate the directed, conscious aboutness of human mental states. | Regional Context: 🌍 Global
  - Source: [Why Human Intentionality Transcends the Machine](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/why-human-intentionality-transcends-machine-husserls-ai-tim-lehoc) - LinkedIn article (Apr 2024)
- **Mind Matters (2023):** Demonstrated via Markov chain experiment that AI systems are "fundamentally different from human intentionality." When trained on own output, AI degenerates (loses information); humans improve with practice. "ChatGPT's output would become better and better the more it consumed of its own output... [but] the Markov chain degenerated into a repetitive sequence." | Regional Context: 🌍 Global
  - Source: [AI vs. Human Intentionality](https://mindmatters.ai/2023/03/ai-vs-human-intentionality) - Mind Matters (Mar 2023)
- **Searle's Chinese Room** (classic): The standard philosophical argument that symbol manipulation (which is what AI does) doesn't constitute understanding or intentionality. A system can process symbols correctly without understanding what they mean. | Regional Context: 🌍 Global
- **Stanford Law's Julian Nyarko** (from MIT Tech Review): "The models may lack a mental model of the world — the ability to simulate a scenario and predict what will happen — and that capability could be at the heart of complex legal reasoning." | Regional Context: 🌍 Global

**Confidence level:** Strong — the "AI can't intend" claim is well-supported across multiple philosophical traditions (phenomenology, analytic philosophy, philosophy of mind). The post's framing is defensible.

**Note:** The post wisely avoids claiming AI will *never* have intentionality — it focuses on the current state. The PhilArchive paper notes "emerging paradigms like neuromorphic computing and embodied AI offer tantalizing possibilities" for the future, but this doesn't undermine the current argument.

## Jurisdictional Flags

⚠️ **"AI replaces junior lawyers" data** — Almost entirely US-centric. Employment statistics (93.4% employment rate, 13% hiring increase) are from US law schools and US firms. Singapore's legal employment market operates differently (smaller pool, different firm structures). The narrative exists in Singapore discourse (PM Wong's Budget 2026 remarks, MinLaw's guide) but the specific data is American.

⚠️ **Copyright and AI training data** — Nearly all litigation is in US courts under US fair use doctrine. Singapore's copyright framework (Copyright Act 2021) differs. The TWS "plagiarised imagery" claim operates in a legal grey zone globally, but the specific legal battles referenced are US-based. Mayer Brown's March 2026 piece on Singapore/HK copyright suggests Asia-Pacific is developing its own frameworks.

⚠️ **Law firm AI bans** — Specific documented examples of blanket AI bans are from US/UK firms. Could not find named Singapore firms with public blanket policies. The MinLaw guide suggests Singapore is moving toward structured adoption rather than prohibition.
