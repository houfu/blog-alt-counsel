# Pitch

**Title:** AI Won't Replace You. Someone Who Decides Will.

**Tags:** AI, Law, Copyright, Article

**Type:** Blog post (long-form, ~9 min read)

**Context:** Part of talk prep on AI, compliance, and the legal profession. Learning in public.

## The Pitch

The conventional AI-and-jobs conversation is stuck on the wrong question — whether AI will replace professionals. This post argues that AI can't practice intentionality at all. It doesn't choose; it resolves statistical patterns. That makes the human behind the prompt the entire moral equation, not part of it. Using The Woke Salaryman's anti-AI art comic as a starting point and photographer Jingna Zhang's copyright fight as the lens, the piece locates the real issue in the difference between choosing eyes closed and eyes open in a portrait — a decision AI never makes. But intentionality isn't inherently good: Zhang's plagiarist was intentional too. The post then turns to legal practice, challenging the "AI replaces juniors, seniors are safe" narrative by arguing there's no such thing as decision-free work — only work where we've stopped noticing the decisions. It ends with practical agency: how any professional, at any level, saves themselves by refusing to let AI be the one that decides.
