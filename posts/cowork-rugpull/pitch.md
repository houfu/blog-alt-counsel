## Title

**I Got Rugpulled by Cowork. The Meter Is the Best Thing to Happen to Lawyer-Builders.**

Backup: "The Free Buffet Is Over. Good."

## Pitch (approved)

> I built a workflow around Microsoft Copilot Cowork. Then, nine days ago, Microsoft pushed it to general availability with usage-based pricing — every model call, tool call, and minute of runtime now metered in Copilot Credits. I got rugpulled. I'm rebuilding, and I can't just walk away, because my colleagues genuinely prefer working in it. Cowork isn't alone — Legora just moved its most capable product off per-seat pricing to consumption too. The consensus is forming fast: the free buffet is over, and that's bad for legal AI. I think that's short-sighted. When every token has a price tag, someone has to decide which legal use cases are worth the spend — and then convince the room they're right. That second part is the hard one. An engineer can cost the workflow; a lawyer can judge the work. Only the lawyer-builder has the confidence to merge both and tell colleagues, "this is trustworthy, and it's the right direction." Metering doesn't make that person less necessary. It makes them the most valuable person in the building.

## Sharpened thesis (from user)

The lawyer-builder's value isn't just *judging* whether a use case is worth the spend — it's **vouching** for it. Colleagues depend on the lawyer-builder to say "this is trustworthy and the right direction." An engineer can cost the workflow but can't carry professional trust; a lawyer can judge the work but lacks the confidence to merge the technical knowledge with professional needs. The lawyer-builder is the only one who can do both — and metering raises the stakes on exactly that trust.

## Verified data (2026-06-25)

- **Cowork GA: June 16, 2026** (9 days before pitch). Usage-based billing in "Copilot Credits" at $0.01/credit; metered on four inputs — model use, context retrieval, tool calls, runtime. PayGo or pre-commit (P3). Runs on Anthropic models (Opus 4.8, Sonnet 4.6) at GA. [Microsoft 365 blog; Neowin; Computerworld]
- **Legora** moved its most capable product (Agent Pro) off per-seat (~$3K/seat/yr, ~$30K min ACV) to consumption-based pricing. *The Lawyer* covered firms "facing a hike in AI cost base." [legora.com/blog/consumption-based-pricing; thelawyer.com]
- **CORRECTION to original pitch idea:** Copilot Studio is *already* consumption-priced (same Copilot Credits, $0.01/credit, pooled, not per-seat). Do NOT frame Studio as a counterexample ("Microsoft didn't even do this with Studio" is FALSE). Use Studio as *evidence of convergence* instead, or lean the "free buffet over" point on Cowork's free preview specifically.

## Pitch interrogation (Step 3.6)

3. **Framing risk:** "Rugpull" toward Microsoft = fair comment, fine. Colleagues handled with respect, no names. Low risk.
