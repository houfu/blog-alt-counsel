# Pitch: "Where Is My Work?" — How AI Tools Quietly Fragment Your Files

## Title

"Where Is My Work?" — How AI Tools Quietly Fragment Your Files

## Pitch

When my laptop went in for repair, I asked Claude to audit my files and check what needed backing up. Git repos, Nextcloud sync, cloud platforms — I assumed I was covered. I'm a lawyer who builds with these tools daily. Then I opened my iPad to work and discovered I was behind on everything: presentation drafts missing, a blog post 30% done stranded in a local folder. One blind spot stung especially: Claude Code on the cloud auto-commits and pushes constantly, so I'd trained myself to trust that my work was saved. When I got /remote working locally, the interface looked identical — but commits weren't happening automatically. The tool's familiar UX masked that my work was living somewhere entirely different. This is frontier pain: Claude Cowork and /remote shipped within weeks of each other, and our security and compliance mental models simply haven't caught up. AI tools multiply the environments where work exists, each with its own assumptions about persistence, and they're changing faster than anyone can update their backup habits. This post maps these new blind spots, connects them to the HBR "Brain Fry" research on cognitive overhead, and offers a practical audit framework for solo counsels who can't afford to lose work.
