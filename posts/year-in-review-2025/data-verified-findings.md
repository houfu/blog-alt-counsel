## THE INFLECTION POINT: October 3, 2025

### üî• The Watershed Article

**"Lawyer ordered to pay client's opponent $800 after discovery of fake case created by AI"**

**What happened:**
- Junior lawyer used AI, generated non-existent case citation
- Senior lawyer failed to supervise and verify
- Assistant Registrar ordered **personal costs of $800**
- Conduct deemed "improper, unreasonable and negligent"

**Why it mattered:**
- First documented professional sanction for AI hallucination in Singapore
- Made abstract risk concrete
- Established senior lawyers' duty to supervise AI use
- Created precedent for future enforcement

**Legal citations:**
- Referenced 2024 Guide on Use of Generative AI Tools
- Echoed warnings from CJ Menon and Justice Aidan Xu
- Set standard: verification is non-negotiable

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR PRACTITIONERS (Findings Section Conclusion)

**The lesson:** Singapore's legal profession moved from "AI as opportunity" to "AI as liability risk" in **8 weeks**.

**Practical implications:**
1. **Verification is now a professional duty** - not optional, not best practice, but mandatory
2. **Senior lawyers are personally accountable** - delegation doesn't absolve supervision
3. **Firm policies have teeth** - from guidance to dismissal in 3 months
4. **Repeat incidents show enforcement is real** - courts are watching, firms are acting

**What changed:** Not the technology, but the consequences. October 3 made the abstract concrete.
