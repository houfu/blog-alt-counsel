**Critical new finding — direct counter-view:** Alexios (GC at Masdar, 12K followers) argues the OPPOSITE of Houfu's thesis: *"If, as General Counsel, you want to understand and control what your AI does with your work product, go for Claude for Word without hesitation."* His argument: because the plugin requires you to build your own skills and playbooks, it forces GCs to understand their AI rather than delegate blindly to a vendor. Jamie Tso commented positively on Alexios's post — a softening of Jamie's January "Word is the wrong substrate" stance.

**Reconciliation path for the draft:** Houfu can distinguish two activities:
- **Building the system** (designing skills/playbooks) = legitimately senior work, Alexios is right
- **Doing ongoing strategic work** (deal view, position-taking, missing-piece thinking) = still belongs in the web interface

This preserves the thesis while engaging honestly with the counter-view.

**Strongly aligned weekend voices (for citation):**
- **Gregory Mostyn (Wexler CEO, Apr 14):** *"Claude for Word is a significant release — and great news for routine legal work. But to understand a case, you don't operate over one document, or a handful."* Literal tactical-vs-strategic articulation.
- **Richard Mabey (Juro CEO, Apr 17):** *"The value in contracting was never really in the drafting."* Mabey argues Word plugin = visible but shallow; value lives downstream.
- **Dhruv Kulkarni (Lumios, Apr 14):** Revision AI is a review-a-junior's-work UX by design. The interface encodes the role.

**Draft beat that changed:** Houfu should no longer claim the tactical/strategic split is contrarian or missing from public takes. It's actually well-represented (Wexler, Juro, Lumios). His contribution is applying it specifically to Claude for Word's interface split (plugin vs web), with the mom's training-deck story as the specific illustration — not the thesis itself.

**Contrarian angle Houfu can claim:** The LegalQuants community's emerging consensus (per LQBrain Apr 11) is markdown-in-IDE (Obsidian + Claude Code + Quartz). Houfu's post offers a third path they haven't foregrounded publicly: **the Claude web interface itself for strategic thinking**. That's the distinctive contribution.
