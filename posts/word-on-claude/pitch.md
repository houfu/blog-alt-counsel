## Pitch

When Anthropic shipped Claude for Word three months after I bet I'd complete 2026 contract reviews without opening Word, my first reaction was: am I about to be very wrong? So I installed Word for the first time in years, just to find out.

The product is good. Easy install, familiar if you've used Word plugins, but the agentic editing is stronger than anything else I've tried in Word. If you came from Claude, sigh of relief. If you live in Word, instruct it and see how far it goes.

The clarifying moment came when I helped my mom with her training deck. Copilot and Meta.ai both flopped. Claude was a huge step up — but the plugin and the web app did different jobs. Big-picture edits were easier in the web app. Accepting inline changes was easier in the plugin.

Here's what that split reveals. If you're using Word + Claude plugin, you're doing junior work. Clause tweaks, minute edits, tactical fixes. Fine for that. But strategic thinking — what the deal actually says, what position you're taking, what's missing — lives in the web interface. Your GC should not be in Word. Prediction #1 holds.

---

## Thesis

**Interface signals (and enables) the level of thinking.**
- Word + Claude plugin = tactical/junior work (clause tweaks, minute edits, inline acceptance)
- Claude web interface = strategic/senior work (deal view, position-taking, missing-piece thinking)

Therefore: workflow transformation still requires leaving the Word view. Prediction #1 from the January 2026 predictions post survives — not because Claude for Word is bad, but because the work that matters for senior lawyers doesn't live inside a document view.
