# Research: Single-Use Tools via Vibecoding & Abstraction Bias in Lawyers

**Date:** 2025-12-23
**Query:** Single-use tools created using vibecoding in Singapore (law-related preferred but not necessary) + Abstraction bias mindset in lawyers
**Regional Focus:** Singapore/ASEAN

---

## Summary

Vibecoding (rapid AI-assisted development) has enabled creation of single-serving software in both legal and non-legal contexts. Rebecca Fordon's work demonstrates legal applications (Boolean search game in 30 minutes, client intake forms with geographic checks), while Singapore's October 2025 hackathon showcased broader applications (AI productivity tools, dating platforms). Research on legal thinking reveals lawyers operate within an abstract/concrete paradox - using precedents and templates for efficiency while needing customization for each unique case. This tension between reusability (abstraction) and bespoke work (specificity) is fundamental to legal practice, but modern AI tools may challenge the traditional balance.

### Theme 2: Abstraction Bias in Legal Thinking

**Abstract/Concrete Paradox:**

- Lawyers activate inconsistent intuitions depending on whether problem is framed abstractly vs. concrete case
- Professional judges show this pattern: decisions differ in abstract vs concrete contexts
- Identifying information about plaintiffs drives the discrepancy
- Legal judgments in concrete cases linked to affective empathy toward plaintiffs
- Source: [Legal decision-making and the abstract/concrete paradox](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0010027720302407) - ScienceDirect | üåç Global research

**Levels of Abstraction in Legal Reasoning:**

- "Level of abstraction refers to the numbers of persons and transactions that generate an issue, to which evidence is true, to which arguments apply, and that are affected by resolution of an issue"
- More persons/transactions = higher abstraction level
- Source: [Levels of Abstraction in Legal Thinking](https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/219376673.pdf) - Cornell eCommons | üåç Global academic research

**Precedents and Case-Based Reasoning:**

- "The basic pattern of legal reasoning is reasoning by example. It is reasoning from case to case."
- Rule-based reasoning has shortcoming: "humans do not think effectively in terms of abstract principles"
- Better understanding when principles expressed as stories/cases rather than abstract rules
- "Generalizations that express what has been learned cannot be separated from the cases they abstract"
- Cases provide meanings for generalizations
- Practitioners employ cases to operate with and on legal concepts
- Source: Multiple legal reasoning sources | üåç Global legal practice

**Templates vs. Bespoke Work Tension:**

- **Efficiency argument**: Templates save time, no need to "reinvent the wheel"
- **Customization requirement**: "Lawyers must review precedents carefully to ensure accuracy and shape them to their facts and current situation"
- **Challenge**: "It can be difficult if systems aren't up to the challenge, and lawyers will quickly lose faith in an out-of-date precedent system"
- **Modern approach**: Technology enables both - templates for efficiency, but customizable for specific needs
- Source: [Legal Document Templates: A Must-Have for Busy Lawyers](https://www.runsensible.com/blog/legal-document-templates-busy-lawyers/) | üåç Global legal practice

**Commoditization Debate:**

- "It makes no sense to have law firm after law firm implementing and attempting to maintain templates for standard kinds of contracts"
- "The substance should be commodities with no secret sauce"
- "It would make sense for law firms to get out of the template business"
- Source: [Yes, Law Firms Do Have a Hard Time Maintaining Template Contracts](https://www.adamsdrafting.com/law-firms-maintaining-template-contracts/) - Adams on Contract Drafting | üåç Global commentary

## Alt-Counsel Angle

**The Single-Serving Revolution for Resource-Constrained Teams:**

Traditional wisdom says solo counsels should maximize reusability - build one template, use it everywhere. But vibecoding flips this: when you can build a bespoke tool in 30 minutes, you don't need everything to generalize.

**Practical implications:**
- **Client intake forms**: Instead of one generic form, build custom intake tools for different practice areas with specific logic (like Fordon's toxic plume map example)
- **Educational tools**: Create interactive explanations for complex legal concepts for specific clients (like Boolean search game adapted for client education)
- **Matter-specific tools**: Build custom checklists, decision trees, or visualizations for individual transactions when stakes justify 30 minutes of setup

**The $50 vs $50K insight:**
- **Old mindset**: Spend months building general platform OR pay $50K for vendor solution
- **New mindset**: Spend 30 minutes building exactly what THIS client needs, RIGHT NOW
- **Cost**: Anthropic/OpenAI API credits (~$0.50-$5 per tool creation) vs months of development or enterprise licensing

**Singapore context:**
- Vibecoding ecosystem well-established (400+ participant hackathons, government-subsidized bootcamps)
- Legal tech hackathons (SMU LIT) creating environment for experimentation
- Solo counsels can learn from broader tech community, not just legal tech vendors

**The abstraction bias connection:**
Both coders (DRY principle) and lawyers (precedent mindset) overvalue reusability. But when creation cost drops to 30 minutes, the calculus changes:
- Don't hesitate to build single-use tools for "regular" clients (not just VIPs)
- Stop optimizing for generalization when specificity serves client better
- Embrace throwaway tools if they solve immediate problem
