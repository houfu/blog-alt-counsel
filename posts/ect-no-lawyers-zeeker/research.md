## Source Case

**JGP v JGQ [2026] SGECT 1**  
Official judgment: https://www.elitigation.sg/gdviewer/s/2026_SGECT_1  
Decision date: 15 May 2026  
Tribunal Magistrate: Jared Kang Chern Wey

Key features for the post:

- First publicly visible ECT decision found in current searches.
- Six related claimants succeeded against the employer after about 40 employees were dismissed over medical-benefit claims.
- The written grounds are unusually long and legally dense for a forum designed around in-person participation.
- The tribunal expressly notes the institutional constraint: parties act in person, so the tribunal does not benefit from opposing counsel’s submissions on legal issues.
- The judgment frames Singapore wrongful dismissal law as layered and unclear after the 2018 transfer of certain Employment Act dismissal claims to the ECT.

## Emerging Commentary Map

The comments split into three frames:

1. **Mainstream news frame**: six employees won compensation because the employer failed to prove dishonesty.
2. **Employment-law frame**: the case exposes unresolved complexity in statutory wrongful dismissal, especially no-reasons dismissals and burden-shifting.
3. **Employer/business reaction frame**: the outcome feels wrong because repeated medical-claim abuse looks intuitively dismissible.

This split is useful for the article. It shows exactly why ECT accessibility is difficult: the same facts produce very different readings depending on whether the reader understands the legal burden and statutory architecture.

## ECT Representation Rules and Forum Design

### Singapore Courts: current public guidance

Source: “Before going to court for an employment claim”  
URL: https://www.judiciary.gov.sg/civil/before-going-to-court-employment-claim  
Read via Jina: 2026-05-20

Key points:

- ECT proceedings are conducted in private in the State Courts.
- Parties must attend case management conferences and ECT hearings.
- Parties must present their own case and cannot engage a lawyer or be represented by another person unless they fall within listed exceptions and obtain ECT approval.
- Entity parties may be represented by officers or full-time employees.
- Certain union members may be represented by a trade union officer.
- Minors may be represented by a parent or guardian.
- A party unable to present their own case by reason of illiteracy or infirmity may be represented by an approved authorised person.
- Where multiple claims by different claimants are heard together, another claimant may represent a party for specified limited matters.
- Except for the listed categories, a party must act in person and cannot be represented by an advocate and solicitor or by a paid/unpaid agent.
- The CCT Friend Scheme may allow a family member, friend, or volunteer from pro bono agencies/entities to accompany a party, subject to approval.

Post use:

This is the doctrinal anchor for the access-asymmetry argument. The system is deliberately lawyer-light, but the permitted representatives for entities mean employers can still appear through internal officers or employees who may be far better resourced than an individual claimant.

### Singapore Courts: filing page

Source: “File an employment claim”  
URL: https://www.judiciary.gov.sg/civil/file-employment-claim  
Read via Jina: 2026-05-20

Key points:

- ECT is described as a speedy and low-cost forum for salary-related and wrongful dismissal claims.
- TADM mediation is compulsory before filing at the ECT.
- The party can file only if mediation remains unresolved and a claim referral certificate has been issued.
- The page states that lawyers are not allowed to represent parties for ECT matters.

Post use:

This supports the “important design” side of the argument: ECT exists to be accessible, not to replicate ordinary civil litigation.

### Singapore Courts: appeal page

Source: “Appeal against an employment claims order made by a tribunal”  
URL: https://www.judiciary.gov.sg/civil/appeal-employment-claims-order-tribunal  
Read via Jina: 2026-05-20

Key points:

- Appeals require leave/permission from the District Court before an appeal to the General Division of the High Court.
- Appeals are limited to questions of law or tribunal jurisdiction.
- The page expressly says that if a party is uncertain whether there is a right to appeal, they should consult a lawyer.
- A lawyer can be hired for the leave/permission application and High Court appeal.

Post use:

This creates an important boundary: the no-lawyer model changes once the dispute becomes appellate and legal-question focused. That supports the article’s claim that legal complexity is not absent; it is managed differently at different stages.

### State Courts launch release, 31 March 2017

Source: “State Courts launch new Employment Claims Tribunals”  
URL: https://www.judiciary.gov.sg/news-and-resources/news/news-details/media-release-state-courts-launch-new-employment-claims-tribunals  
Read via Jina: 2026-05-20

Key points:

- The ECT was launched on 1 April 2017.
- It was designed as a speedy, low-cost forum with simplified procedures and a judge-led process.
- The launch release states that no lawyers would be involved.
- It also states that because of simplified procedures, ECT would not be a forum for complicated employment claims.
- The launch release ties ECT to access to justice: the processes were designed so those in need could navigate and access adjudicatory processes.

Post use:

This is the strongest historical source for the article’s irony. The ECT was explicitly designed as lawyer-free because it was not supposed to handle complicated employment claims. `[2026] SGECT 1` is a case where the tribunal itself says wrongful dismissal law has become layered, difficult, and unclear, and explains that it lacks opposing counsel’s arguments on legal issues because parties act in person.

### TADM mediation guide

Source: “Mediation guide for salary-related claims and employment disputes”  
URL: https://www.tal.sg/tadm/mediation-guide-3  
Read via Jina: 2026-05-20

Key points:

- TADM mediation helps employees and employers resolve employment disputes amicably.
- Lawyers are not allowed to represent any party in mediation.
- TADM mediators are trained to handle employment disputes and many have HR and industrial relations experience.
- TADM mediation may take place online or in person; sessions can take up to 3 hours and the process may take about 8 weeks.
- TADM says only the employee, employer, and mediator can participate in mediation; third parties such as lawyers, family, or friends are not allowed.
- For employers, authorised mediation representatives include office holders or full-time employees with authorisation.
- TADM identifies wrongful dismissal claims as including dismissal without just or sufficient cause, and says wrongful dismissal excludes dismissal due to misconduct, poor performance, or redundancy if contractual notice was served or paid in lieu.
- TADM directs users to MOM’s wrongful dismissal guide and the Tripartite Guidelines on Wrongful Dismissal.
- TADM says Ask TADM can provide immediate answers, claim assessment, and help to file a claim.

Post use:

TADM adds the pre-ECT layer to the same asymmetry. Lawyers are excluded from mediation too, but employers can still respond through internal representatives. The mediation guide is also much simpler than `[2026] SGECT 1`, which highlights the gap between public-facing process guidance and the legal complexity once an actual dispute reaches written grounds.

## Source Case: Internal Quote That Supports the Article

Zeeker fragment lookup retrieves `[2026] SGECT 1` at [4] directly by querying `judgments_fragments` for the case `source_url` and searching for `act in-person`.

At [4], Tribunal Magistrate Jared Kang says the six related cases demonstrated the difficulty and lack of clarity in wrongful dismissal law. He writes that he is working within an institutional setting where parties are required to act in person and tribunals therefore do not benefit from opposing counsel’s arguments, especially on matters of law. He nevertheless considered it crucial to write an exposition because the law needs clarification given the high volume of wrongful dismissal claims and the risk of inconsistent outcomes.

Zeeker also retrieves Annex 3 at A.48, where the tribunal says the label “wrongful dismissal” now covers two different things: the common law action and the statutory claim under s 14(2) of the Employment Act. The tribunal says the six related cases exposed that boundaries between substance, process, forum, and remedy are not consistently understood.

Post use:

This paragraph should be the centre of the public argument. The article does not need to overstate the critique; the judgment itself identifies the tension.
